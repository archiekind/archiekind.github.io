About Me

I'm Archie Kind, a Computer Science student at the University of Bristol with a passion for technology and the future of computing. My interests range from machine learning to embedded software development, and I am continually expanding my skills. Welcome to my personal website!

Industry Experience

During 2022-2023, I participated in the GKN Automotive Kevin Rye Memorial Skills Initiative, a program spanning five weeks over the course of a year. Building on an earlier work experience opportunity with GKN Automotive, this placement provided hands-on exposure to industry-specific tools such as MoTec for controlling valves and pumps, as well as technologies like Controlled Area Networking (CAN). Collaborating with the engineering team, I contributed to extending the software stack on test rigs by developing a footage recording and logging system. This system enabled remote viewing by streaming video over the local area network (LAN).

University Course Work

During my time at university, I have invested significant effort in extending myself through coursework, going far beyond the requirements. One notable project was a C program for converting and compressing image files using a custom file format. By employing 2D run-length encoding and various self-developed optimizations, I achieved a formidable compression ratio of 6.03. Another significant project involved designing a hardware implementation of the PRESENT encryption algorithm. I extended this project by creating separate combinatorial, sequential, and pipelined designs while conducting extensive research into Verilog efficiency.

Bristol HyPower Rocketry

As the Software Team Lead for Bristol HyPower Rocketry during the 2024/25 development cycle, I am guiding the development of embedded systems for our rocket. We are competing in the European Rocketry Challenge, hosted by the Portuguese Space Agency in October, with a target apogee of 3 km. This role has deepened my leadership and low-level software development skills, giving me valuable experience managing teams.

Extended Project Qualification (a research-based academic program)

I researched and analysed recent developments in language modelling, and then produced a 5000 word report focusing on the SwiGLU activation, grouped-query attention (GQA) and reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF). To collect primary data for my report I built a 25m parameter language model to conduct ablation tests.
